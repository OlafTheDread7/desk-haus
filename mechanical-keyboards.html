How to choose a mechanical keyboard

Switches set the feel and the noise

Linear switches (like reds) are smooth and quiet, great for typing in shared spaces. Tactile switches (browns) give a gentle bump for feedback without much sound. Clicky switches (blues) are loud and satisfying but will annoy a partner on calls. A hot-swap board lets you change your mind later without soldering — a big reason we favor them.

Pick a layout that fits your work

A 75% or TKL (tenkeyless) layout saves desk space and keeps your mouse closer to home, which is easier on your shoulder. If you live in spreadsheets, a full-size board with a number pad earns its footprint. Compact 60% boards are the most minimal but drop the function row and arrows, so be sure you won't miss them.

Wired or wireless?

Wireless boards keep your desk clean and travel well, and modern battery life easily lasts weeks. Wired connections give you the lowest latency and never need charging, which competitive gamers still prefer. Many of our picks do both, so you get a clean desk most of the time and a cable option when you want zero lag.
