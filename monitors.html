How to choose a monitor

Match resolution to screen size

On a 27-inch monitor, 4K gives you razor-sharp text and plenty of workspace, while 1440p hits a great balance of clarity and high refresh rates for gaming. Avoid 4K on smaller 24-inch panels (everything gets tiny without scaling) and avoid 1080p at 27 inches, where text starts to look soft. The panel size and resolution should be chosen together.

USB-C with power delivery is the quiet game-changer

If you work from a laptop, a monitor with USB-C power delivery and a built-in hub lets one cable carry video, charge your laptop, and connect peripherals. It turns your display into a docking station and cleans up your desk instantly. Check the wattage matches your laptop's charging needs.

Refresh rate only matters for motion

A 144Hz or 165Hz panel makes gaming and scrolling feel buttery, but for spreadsheets and writing, a standard 60Hz 4K panel with great color is the better spend. Decide whether you're optimizing for fast motion or for crisp, accurate detail — chasing both pushes you into expensive territory.
