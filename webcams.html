How to choose a webcam

1080p is plenty — 4K is a bonus

For video calls, a good 1080p camera looks sharp and uses far less bandwidth and CPU than 4K. Step up to 4K only if you'll crop or zoom into the image, record high-quality video, or simply want the most detail possible. A great 1080p sensor beats a mediocre 4K one every time, so judge the camera, not just the resolution number.

Lighting beats megapixels

The fastest way to look better on camera is to fix your lighting, not buy a pricier webcam. Face a window or add a soft light in front of you and even a budget camera shines. If your room is genuinely dark and you can't add light, choose a camera with a large sensor and good low-light handling like the Kiyo Pro.

Don't overlook the microphone and mounting

Many webcams double as your mic, so noise-reducing dual mics save you from buying a separate one for casual calls. Also check that the clip fits your monitor and that there's a tripod thread or a privacy shutter if those matter to you — small ergonomics that make a camera pleasant to live with.
